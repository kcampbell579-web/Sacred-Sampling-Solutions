Browse by Contaminant

Learn about what we test for.

Nine of the most common — and most consequential — contaminants found in U.S. homes.

Heavy metal

Lead

From older pipes, solder, and fixtures. No safe level for kids.

 Air & water

VOCs

Solvents and fuels that off-gas and dissolve into water.

 Gas

Radon

An invisible soil gas and a leading cause of lung cancer.

 Heavy metal

Arsenic

Naturally in bedrock and wells; long-term cancer risk.

 Agricultural

Nitrates

From fertilizer and septic; especially risky for infants.

 Mineral fiber

Asbestos

Legacy insulation and materials that release harmful fibers.

 Forever chemicals

PFAS

Synthetic "forever chemicals" that persist in the body.

 Biological

Mold

Thrives after leaks and floods; affects air and health.

 Bacteria

E. coli

A marker of fecal contamination in wells and water.
